Ceremony · Foundational Reading

Safety in the Body

How to orient within intense somatic experience during ceremony, what you may feel, how to work with it, and what our team is here to support.

Iboga Journey

The deepest medicine arrives through the body. This guide is here to remind you that sensation, even intense sensation, is the path itself. Meeting it is the work.

What You May Feel

The Language of the Body

Iboga ceremony is a full-body experience. Your system is being invited to open, release, and reorganize, and sensation will move. Some of it will feel familiar. Some of it will feel new or intense. All of it belongs.

What follows is a partial map of what can arise. Every sensation on this map is a sign that your system is doing exactly what it was built to do.

Physical

Heaviness, vibration, heat or cold, tingling

Your body may feel like it weighs a great deal, or like it is vibrating from the inside. Waves of warmth or cold can move through you. Limbs may feel electric or slightly numb. These are all common sensations as the nervous system shifts state and the body opens.

Involuntary

Shaking, trembling, yawning, tears, sound

The body knows how to discharge. Trembling, shaking, deep yawns, spontaneous tears, and unexpected sounds are how tension and held experience move out. Let them. What wants to move will move more easily when you stop trying to hold it still.

Emotional

Waves of grief, joy, fear, love, longing

Emotions often arrive in waves rather than as thoughts. You may weep without a story attached. You may laugh without reason. You may feel a deep tenderness, or a fierce grief, or a pure and inexplicable joy. Let each wave rise and pass. Honor it simply by letting it move through.

Perceptual

Time softening, imagery, visions, inner sound

In Iboga ceremony, perception shifts. Time may stretch or fold. Imagery, memories, archetypal visions, and inner sounds may arise. Some moments will feel luminous and clear; others will feel dreamlike and strange. Let each one come as it comes, and let it pass when it is ready.

Mental

The fight-flight-freeze voice

Your thinking mind may try to keep you safe in the way it always has, by narrating, controlling, analyzing, doubting, or wanting to leave. This is the survival part of you doing its job. Let it speak, thank it for its care, and return to your breath. The deeper wisdom will take over from there.

Working With Intensity

Five Principles for Meeting What Arises

These are simple. They will stay with you. Whenever the body feels overwhelming, one of these will meet you where you are.

One

When in doubt, do less

The temptation in intensity is to try harder, push through, or fix. The body is already doing its work, it asks only for space. Soften the jaw, soften the belly, let the shoulders drop. Do less and let the medicine do more.

Two

Move with what is moving

If the body wants to shake, let it shake. If sound wants to come, let it come. If breath wants to deepen or quicken, follow it. Trauma and held experience move through us with breath, sound, and movement. Allowing the movement is how you pass through, the movement itself is the medicine.

Three

Return to the breath

Your breath is the most available anchor you have. It is always with you, steady, simple, yours. When sensation becomes disorienting, find one slow inhale. Then one slow exhale. One breath is enough. The next one will come.

Four

Practice alone together

You are in a shared container with other members, and you are on your own sacred thread. Stay with your own experience. Keep your attention close to your body and trust that your support team is holding the whole room. Your journey is yours. Theirs is theirs. Both are held.

Five

Trust the wisdom of your body

Your body has carried you through every experience of your life. It knows how to open, how to close, how to discharge, how to rest. It has wisdom your mind will never fully grasp. When the mind is uncertain, ask the body. It will answer in sensation, in impulse, in a sense of yes or no. Listen.

An Anchor You Can Return To

The Heart-Belly Breath

This practice is your most reliable resource. It calms the nervous system, reminds the body of its own safety, and can be done in any position, lying down, seated, in ceremony, in the moments after.

One hand on your heart. One hand on your belly. Let your shoulders drop.

7
Inhale for seven. Fill the belly first, then let the breath rise into the chest. Feel both hands expand.
7
Hold for seven. Gentle and soft. Let the body be full.
7
Exhale for seven. With audible sound. Let the chest collapse. Let the belly soften. Release what is ready to go.

Repeat as many rounds as you need. One round is enough. Ten rounds will meet you deeper. There is no wrong way to do this.

Communication in Ceremony

How to Signal the Team

You are held throughout ceremony. Our facilitators are trained to read the body, notice subtle shifts, and offer support while honoring your process. You also have agency, you can communicate what you need, at any time, in simple ways.

Simple Signals

  • Peace sign If you need to leave the space, to use the bathroom, get water, step outside, raise a peace sign so the team knows. You will be supported getting where you need to go.
  • Raised hand If you need something specific, a facilitator's presence, grounding touch, a cool cloth, reassurance, raise your hand. Someone will come to you.
  • Words You are welcome to speak. You can say "I need support," "I want to be held," "I need space," "I am overwhelmed." Simple words are enough. We will meet you with whatever you need.
  • Silence Silence is welcome. If you are turned inward and the work is moving through you, stay with it. We will check on you gently and honor the space you are in.

Everything we invite is a suggestion and a guide. The deepest instruction always comes from the wisdom of your own body.

You Are Held

What Our Team is Here For

You are held by a constellation of facilitators, assistants, and prayer. Our team is trained, present, and constantly attuned to the container as a whole and to each individual within it.

Throughout ceremony, your team offers all of this

  • Continuous attentive presence, someone is always watching the room with care
  • Grounding touch when welcomed, a hand on the shoulder, the feet, the crown
  • Physical support, water, rest, temperature regulation, movement assistance
  • Somatic guidance, invitations to breathe, to sound, to move, or simply to be still
  • Emotional accompaniment, your tears, your fear, and your joy are all welcome here
  • Safety of the container, energetic protection, ritual holding, and the prayers of the lineage
  • Intervention if needed, in the rare moments when something medical or acute arises, we respond immediately and with full skill

Your only task is to be present with what arises in you. We are holding the rest.

Trust the wisdom of your body. Trust the constellation holding you. Trust that what is arising is arising for your healing. You are exactly where you need to be.
