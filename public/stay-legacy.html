Community-Held Homes

Rooted in
Aloha

Something rare happens when people who deeply love a place open their doors. The homes that hold our guests in Hanalei are offerings, chosen by members of our community who know this work, trust this mission, and want to play a part in the healing that happens here.

Each home sits in Hanalei, steps from the bay, cradled by the Nā Pali mountains, brushed by the mist of waterfalls. You wake up here and the land is already working.

"These homes are the first layer of the medicine, arriving somewhere that was prepared for you with love, in a sacred place."

Each guest placement is thoughtful. During your intake, we ask about any special needs, preferences, or considerations so we can match you with the space that fits you best.

Guests share a spacious, welcoming home with other participants, or, for groups of three or more arriving together, the home is yours as a private container for your group. We host small, intimate gatherings of five or fewer at a time, so the space and the care remain deeply personal.

Everything Is Held

What's
Included

Your Private Sanctuary

A private room or suite within a community-held North Shore home, clean, nature-integrated, and prepared with care. Your own space to rest, reflect, and integrate between sessions.

ʻĀina Nourishment

Farm-to-table meals sourced from Kauaʻi's living land. High-vibration, deeply nourishing, and aligned with your protocol, whether that is a full detox cleanse, gentle whole foods, or ceremonial fasting support.

Arrival & Departure

Arrival pickup and departure drop-off are available through us, simply let us know during intake and we will have everything coordinated. Guests are also welcome to arrange their own transportation.

Private Sessions & Ceremonies

Iboga ceremony, breathwork, somatic sessions, sound healing, and integration work, held privately in our dedicated space or in nature itself. The container is yours. The land is the temple.

Nature Immersion Daily

Ocean swims, river floats, waterfall hikes, grounding practices, woven into your days with intention. Kauaʻi's wild North Shore is the primary medicine.

Full-Spectrum Support

Our team is with you across the arc of your journey. Text support, check-ins, and the quiet reassurance of knowing someone who genuinely cares is always close.

Guests are also welcome to arrange their own accommodations and meals in Hanalei, we are glad to offer recommendations.

What to Expect

The Arc of
Your Stay

Every journey takes its own shape. There is a rhythm to arriving here, moving through the work, and transitioning back home changed. Here is the shape of what most guests experience.

01
Arrival

Landing & Settling In

Your pickup is arranged in advance, or you are welcome to make your own way north. Your home is ready when you arrive, clean, quiet, and waiting. A nourishing meal, an orientation, and the chance to simply feel what it is like to be here.

02
Opening

Time on the Land & in the Water

Mornings in nature, your discovery conversation, and the days of sacred preparation before ceremony. Your protocol and your nervous system shape what unfolds.

03
The Middle Days

Ceremony & Presence

The heart of the journey, Iboga ceremony, somatic sessions, nature immersion, and the quiet between where the deepest transformation moves through you. We hold you close across every moment of it.

04
Return

Integration & Closing

The final days are for consolidating what Iboga has opened in you, integration sessions, a closing ceremony, and a clear path forward. You leave as someone who has been met by the medicine and changed by it.

Optional Weaving

Private Container.
Living Community.

Your journey at Vital Kauaʻi is held privately, at your pace, in your own sacred container. That is the foundation.

And yet Hanalei has something rare and genuine: a real, living community of practitioners, teachers, surfers, farmers, and healers who chose this place for the same reasons you did. When you feel ready, that world is right outside the door.

For those who feel called, we offer the option to step beyond the private container and move through our community, attending a class, sitting in a ceremony circle, sharing a meal, or simply feeling what it is like to belong somewhere for a few days. Integration happens in relationship, and this community has a way of receiving people exactly as they are.

The Living ʻĀina

Become One
With the Land

The North Shore carries a living tradition of people who have loved and tended this land for generations. These are the stewards, gardens, and community anchors that make this place sacred, and they welcome those who arrive with open hands. We weave these experiences into your stay for those who feel called to go deeper into the ʻāina.

Hanalei Bay · North Shore

Waipā Foundation

Living Ahupuaʻa · Hanalei Bay

A 1,600-acre living ahupuaʻa along Hanalei Bay, wetland taro fields, orchards, canoes, and a weekly poi day keep ancient Hawaiian values alive and practiced.

Visit Waipā →

Hāʻena · North Shore

Limahuli Garden

National Tropical Botanical Garden · Hāʻena

A puʻuhonua, place of refuge, in one of the most biodiverse valleys in Hawaiʻi. Ancient taro terraces, native forest, and rare plants tended for over 1,500 years.

Visit Limahuli →

Hāʻena · North Shore

Hui Makaʻāinana o Makana

Community Stewards · Hāʻena

A family-based community in Hāʻena stewarding the reef, watershed, and cultural memory of this place, and established Hawaiʻi's first Community-Based Subsistence Fishing Area.

Visit Hui Makaʻāinana →

Hāʻena · End of the Road

Hāʻena State Park

Kēʻē Beach · Kalalau Trailhead · North Shore

Kēʻē Beach, ancient sea caves, and the Kalalau Trailhead along the Nā Pali Coast. Sacred archaeological sites and restored loʻi kalo await. Reservations open 30 days in advance.

Before You Ask

Common
Questions

If something specific is on your mind, bring it. Reach out, every question matters when you are preparing to do real work.

Most guests stay an average of 10 days, though stays vary depending on the depth of the work they are here to do. During your intake, we will discuss what length of container feels right for what you are moving through.

The first step is a discovery call, a real conversation with Rachel and/or Josh, bookable directly through our Calendly. We want to understand what brings you here, what you are carrying, your health history, and what support will serve you best. This is how we begin to know you, so that the container we hold for you is built for who you actually are.

Yes. We work with couples and close companions who wish to move through a journey together. Co-journeying can be deeply powerful, and it does require its own kind of preparation and intentionality. Let us know during intake that you are coming with someone, and we will discuss whether shared or separate containers will serve you both best. We also welcome groups, intimate gatherings of friends, family, or community who feel called to transform together. Reach out and we will shape something worthy of the occasion.

Light, natural fabrics that can get wet and get dirty. Layers for cool mornings and evenings. Good walking shoes and flip flops. A journal. Anything that helps you feel at home in your body. Your full packing and preparation guide is available in your member portal once your journey is confirmed.

Meals are prepared with the same intentionality as everything else at Vital Kauaʻi. We source locally and seasonally, farms, farmers' markets, and the ocean contribute to what ends up on your plate. All dietary needs, allergies, and protocol-specific requirements are gathered during intake and honored throughout your stay. Whether you are on a full cleanse, a gentle whole-foods protocol, or simply eating in alignment with the work, your nutrition is held with care.

Līhuʻe Airport (LIH) is approximately one hour from Hanalei along Kauaʻi's scenic North Shore highway. Yes, WiFi is available in the homes. Many guests find that their relationship with devices naturally shifts once they are here, Hanalei has a way of drawing you fully into the present.
